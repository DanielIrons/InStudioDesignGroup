Wendy began her interior design/project management career in Dallas/Fort Worth in 2002. She has designed for clients all over Texas and the United States. Her projects range from small room changes to complete remodels, with special attention given to details, style, budgets, and deadlines. She works with clients to create rooms that they will enjoy for years to come. Wendy grew up in military communities and traveled the globe with her father and later with her husband, eventually settling in beautiful San Antonio, Texas. She is a graduate of the University of Texas at San Antonio, Business College and excelled in a specialized design program at St. Mary's University. Wendy's diverse educational background and her global experiences have resulted in a unique and unrestricted approach to design.

Why do I need an interior designer?

Whether you need a little help or a lot, from answers to a few questions on color selection or furniture layout to help on planning and completing a complicated remodel or new construction project, the advice and guidance of an Interior Designer is invaluable. Having a professional Interior Designer on your team who has the knowledge and experience of countless similar projects as well as the information and expertise for finding the exact products and trades-people you need to get the best result for your project is priceless. Working with an Interior Designer will help you to move seamlessly and efficiently through the project, as well as help you prevent costly mistakes or side-steps along the way.

Is a budget necessary?

Absolutely, the budget is essential for planning and designing your space without time costly revisions. This saves time and we can get you into your finished room as quickly as possible. The budget is the difference between designing (for example) a $150,000 kitchen and a $15,000 kitchen which goes without saying is quite an enormous difference.

Why do you have a design fee and purchasing fee on the product?

The design fee is for design concepts, solutions to the problems, and advice on any purchases. When a product is ordered through the designer, there is a great deal of time spent on producing purchase orders, ordering cuttings of fabrics, and wall-coverings from current stock for approval. Our procurement fee is 25-30% plus the cost of goods sold. As professionals we have access to wholesale vendors from whom we have built a relationship with over time to ensure over quality control of your purchased items before they arrive to you. Additionally, time is spent on coordinating deliveries and generally ensuring that the whole process runs smoothly until the item is delivered to the client. The purchasing fee covers this entire process and assures the clients' peace of mind. In addition, to having insider accesses to products you still save with designer discount and are typically not subject to full retail costs.
